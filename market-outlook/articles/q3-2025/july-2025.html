Market Overview – July 2025

Navigating Policy Clarity and Fiscal Pressure

July 31, 2025 10 min read Tulga-Ochir Sugar

July 2025 was a month of significant transition, where the resolution of short-term policy uncertainties gave way to a growing awareness of a long-term fiscal and liquidity crisis. Markets began the month fixated on the finalization of new trade tariffs and landmark crypto legislation. While clarity on these fronts, coupled with easing geopolitical tensions, provided a tailwind for risk assets, this optimism was increasingly tempered by the looming reality of a massive flood of Treasury issuance and signs of a brewing government funding battle. This backdrop placed the Federal Reserve on a precarious tightrope, caught between sticky inflation and a weakening economy, all while equities climbed to new highs in a fragile, low-volatility environment.

Policy Overhangs Resolved, For Now

The first half of the month was defined by the resolution of two major sources of market uncertainty:

Tariff Finalization

The Trump administration clarified its new tariff list on July 9. While the economic impact remains a concern, the finalization removed a significant risk premium associated with policy ambiguity, allowing corporations to plan with greater certainty.

"Crypto Week" Delivers Landmark Legislation

The U.S. Congress made significant strides in digital asset regulation. The GENIUS Act, a comprehensive framework for stablecoins, passed the Senate and was poised for adoption by the House. This move was seen as a major win for the industry, providing legal clarity and creating a new, structural source of demand for short-term U.S. Treasuries, which are mandated as backing for regulated stablecoins.

Adding to the risk-on mood, reports of progress in Gaza ceasefire negotiations helped lower the geopolitical risk premium that had been weighing on global markets.

The Looming Liquidity Squeeze and Fiscal Strain

While short-term risks subsided, a more formidable long-term challenge came into sharp focus. Following the passage of the "Big Beautiful Bill," the U.S. Treasury began the process of replenishing its Treasury General Account (TGA). This set the stage for an estimated $800 billion in new bond issuance over the next two quarters, creating a significant liquidity drain from the financial system as capital is reallocated from risk assets to government debt.

This pressure was compounded by a brewing fiscal crisis. The TGA balance steadily declined throughout the month, falling to approximately $333 billion by late July. With a September 30 government funding deadline approaching, the risk of a political showdown and potential government shutdown has risen significantly, threatening to weigh on consumer and business confidence in the months ahead.

The Federal Reserve's Tightrope Walk

The Federal Reserve found itself in an increasingly difficult position, navigating conflicting economic signals and rising political pressure. Key data points painted a confusing picture:

Sticky Inflation

June's CPI and PPI reports surprised to the upside (0.3% MoM for both), suggesting that inflation, driven by tariffs and energy costs, was not fully contained.

Weakening Economy

The housing sector showed clear signs of a downturn, with homebuilder sentiment falling to recessionary levels and mortgage rates hovering at a restrictive 6.75%.

Ahead of its July 30 meeting, the market priced in a 95% probability of the Fed holding rates steady. However, dissent within the FOMC was reportedly growing, setting the stage for a potential rate cut in September. This delicate balancing act was made more challenging by the increasing politicization of monetary policy, highlighted by public disagreements between Fed Chair Powell and President Donald Trump, which introduced a new layer of uncertainty and risk for bond markets.

A Market Priced for Perfection

Despite the looming fiscal and liquidity challenges, equity markets demonstrated remarkable resilience. The S&P 500 hit all-time highs four times in the final week of July, with its forward P/E multiple exceeding a lofty 22.7x. This rally occurred in an environment of extremely low volatility (VIX) and tight credit spreads, suggesting a market "priced for perfection" and highly vulnerable to any potential shock.

The brief spike of the 30-year Treasury yield above 5% served as a stark reminder of the market's sensitivity to concerns over future debt supply and political stability.

The outlook heading into August is one of cautious optimism overshadowed by significant structural headwinds. While markets have cheered policy clarity, the true test will be their ability to absorb the coming wave of Treasury supply and navigate the inevitable political brinkmanship around government funding in the fall.
